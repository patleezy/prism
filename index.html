Tips for better results
Be specific — include price points, product details, or specific claims to test
Use Concept Testing for new ideas, Price Sensitivity when validating a price point
Results are directional, not definitive — use to guide decisions, not replace real research
Save sessions to compare findings across multiple runs
Works for personal decisions too
Choosing between colleges, neighborhoods, or job offers — test perception, not just facts
Salary negotiation — how does this offer read to a hiring manager, a peer, or a recruiter?
Big life decisions often hinge on how others perceive the options — Prism surfaces that
Pair Prism's perception layer with real data (stats, reviews) for the full picture
Concept Testing
Best for evaluating new product ideas, features, or services before investing in development.
e.g. "We're launching a $49/mo subscription that replaces your gym. Would you switch?"
Price Sensitivity
Uses Van Westendorp methodology — each persona evaluates your price across four thresholds to find an acceptable range.
e.g. "At what price does this feel too cheap, fair, expensive, or way too expensive?"
Message Testing
Test ad copy, taglines, or positioning. Each persona rates on clarity, relevance, and motivation to act.
e.g. "Which tagline lands better: 'Sound that disappears' or 'The last headphone you'll ever need'?"
Open Research
Free-form exploration, AI-moderated. Best for discovery or questions that don't fit the other types.
e.g. "Walk me through how you decide to buy a new pair of headphones."
